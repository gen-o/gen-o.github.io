Abstract

GENO is an ontology for the field of Genetic Criticism and Editing, that makes possible to describe all kinds of avant-textes, their relationships in and outside the genetic dossier and with the publications.

Avant-textec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/hum/geno/AvantTexte

A manuscript, typescript or printed text with manuscript evidence kept in an archive or private collection. It is part of a genetic dossier as avantextual material for a project of publication, achieved or not. It can be part of the pre-redactional (note, plan, list) or redactional phase (draft, clean copy, final manuscript) of the genetic process.

is in domain of
avant-texte is before avant-texteop, avant-texte is part of genetic dossierop, has genetic stagedp
is in range of
avant-texte is before avant-texteop
is disjoint with
Diaryc, Publicationc

An example of an avant-texte is a draft or a plan.

An example of diary is an entry in the agenda.

Genetic dossierc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/hum/geno/GeneticDossier

The genetic dossier includes all the avant-textes of a work, which has been published or conceived for publication.

is in domain of
genetic dossier results in publicationop
is in range of
avant-texte is part of genetic dossierop, diary is reused in genetic dossierop, is reused inop, publication is reused in genetic dossierop

Examples of genetic dossiers are the following: all the lists, plans, drafts of the book 'Air de la solitude' (1945) are part of its genetic dossier; all the lists, plans, draft of 'Cendre' in Aujourd'hui (1930-07) are part of its genetic dossier.

Examples of publications are an article published in a newspaper, a book, an anthology.

avant-texte is before avant-texteop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/hum/geno/avantTexteisBeforeAvantTexte

A document comes before another, chronologically, in the genetic dossier or avant-texte.

has domain
Avant-textec
has range
Avant-textec

Some examples of the use of the property are: the draft CLSR GR MS 1 H1/1a precedes the draft CLSR GR MS 1 H1/2a in the genetic dossier of 'Dédicace' in Schweizer Annalen (1935-11); the draft CLSR GR MS 1 H1/3a precedes the clean copy CLSR GR MS 1 H1/3b in the genetic dossier of 'Deux moments d'une quête' in Suisse Contemporaine (1941-11).

avant-texte is part of genetic dossierop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

Some examples of the use of the property are: the avant-texte CLSR HLM D 1 ROU 4 is part of the genetic dossier of 'Feuillets' in 'Écrits' (1950); the avant-texte CLSR GR MS 1 H1/1a is part of the genetic dossier of 'Dédicace' in Schweizer Annalen (1935-11).

diary is before diaryop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/hum/geno/diaryIsBeforeDiary

A diary entry is chronologically before a new copy of the same diary entry. It happens when the author copies one or more diary entries with or without modification in a new support.

has domain
Diaryc
has range
Diaryc

Some examples of the use of the property are: a diary entry on a loose sheet is copied with few modifications in a notebook; a diary entry in a pocket notebook is copied without modifications in a notebook.

genetic dossier results in publicationop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

is reused inop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/hum/geno/isReusedInGeneticDossier

An abstract property, indicating the reuse of a diary entry or of a publication in the genetic dossier for a new project. It is not to be used as such, choose a sub-property instead.

has sub-properties

Genetic dossierc

publication is reused in genetic dossierop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/hum/geno/publicationIsReusedInGeneticDossier

A publication is reused in the genetic dossier of a new text.

has super-properties
is reused inop
has domain
Publicationc
has range
Genetic dossierc

Examples of the use of the property are: the publication 'Deux moments d'une quête' in Suisse Contemporaine (1941-11) is reused in the genetic dossier of 'D'une quête' in Botteghe Oscure (1950-05); the publication 'Cendre' in Aujourd'hui (1930-07) is partially reused in the genetic dossier of the collection 'Air de la solitude' (1945); the publication 'Feuillets' (1929) in reissued with modifications in 'Feuillets' in 'Écrits' (1950).

Data Properties

has genetic stagedp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/hum/geno/hasGeneticStage

The genetic stage of a document. Suggested values are note, plan, list, draft, clean copy, final manuscript, annotated proofs, annotated publication; other or different values can be conceived for different projects.

has domain
Avant-textec
has range
literal

Some examples of the use of the property are: the document CLSR GR MS 1 H1/3b is a clean copy as genetic stage; the document CLSR GR MS 1 H1/4f is an annotated proof as genetic stage.
